Wearable device shell

1/15/23
It's basically a smart, low-cost device that detects carotid artery stenosis through intensity variation analysis for cerebrovascular accident prevention.

Ehan Masud and Aneeq Chowdhury did all the research and figuring out how to detect carotid artery in your neck for their science fair. I only take credit in for creating and designing the model that they used. As you can see in the picture above this model holds a camera and light source so that you can see the neck and stuff. Foam was added for comfort purposes.
